The Atari 2600 (1977) was a pioneering home video game console that popularized the use of cartridges to play a variety of different games.
The NES (1985) Helped revive the video game industry after the North American video game crash of 1983, and introduced iconic characters such as Mario and Zelda.
The Sega Genesis/Mega Drive (1988) popularized the "console wars" between Sega and Nintendo, and introduced popular franchises such as Sonic the Hedgehog, featuring a 16-bit processor.
The Gameboy (1989) was the first successful handheld video game console, paving the way for future portable gaming systems and introducing classics like Tetris and Pokemon.
